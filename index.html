About This Portfolio

Keene Kolbeinsen-Tafaoa

This portfolio demonstrates my journey learning how to use AI in business-related settings, and shows how I applied that learning through hands-on labs for MIS 432.

At the beginning of the course, I did not know much about AI. I was also resistant to it — I'd been told it would eliminate necessary jobs by outperforming humans. But each lab changed my thinking. I was able to demonstrate the real benefits of using AI while also honestly acknowledging its limits: it can't understand the full context of every situation, which is why a human is always needed to make the final decision.

What follows is a record of seven companies, seven systems, and one consistent lesson — AI is a tool for augmentation, not replacement.

What I Learned to See
  • How real companies structure AI as an operational pipeline, not a one-off feature
  • Why data quality and pipeline design determine outcomes more than model choice
  • How governance and human oversight are built into production AI systems
  • Where AI creates business value — and where it falls short without human judgment

Big-Picture Insights

Key Takeaways

01

AI Is Only as Good as Its Data Pipeline

Across every company — from Spotify to Waymo — the quality of training data and the integrity of the data pipeline determined outcomes far more than model architecture. Garbage in, garbage out is not a cliché; it is the first engineering constraint of every production AI system.

02

Every AI System Needs a Human-in-the-Loop Governance Plan

Whether it's a physician reviewing an ambient documentation summary or a hospital employee auditing an AI-drafted insurance appeal, the human is not optional overhead. They are the accountability layer. Removing them doesn't make the system more efficient — it makes it unaccountable.

03

Business Value Comes from Workflow Integration, Not Model Accuracy

A model with 95% accuracy that isn't embedded into a real decision-making workflow creates zero business value. The companies that win with AI — Netflix, Uber, Airbnb — have built their entire operational loop around the model's output. The model is the business, not an add-on to it.

04

AI Augments; Context Remains Human

I came into this course skeptical of AI because I feared it would replace human judgment. What I found instead is that AI struggles with context — it can't always know what matters in a specific situation. The real pattern across every lab: AI handles scale and pattern recognition, humans supply context and make final calls.
